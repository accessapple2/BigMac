QUESTION 3: FILL LOGIC
==============================================================

Source file: engine/super_backtest_v5.py

Signal detection (lines 340–355):
    Uses sub["Close"].values where m = (df.index <= day)
    px = float(c[-1])  →  today's closing price

Fill price (lines 568-569):
    entry_px = px * (1 + SLIPPAGE + EXEC_DELAY)
    = Close[-1] * 1.0025

VERDICT: SAME-DAY CLOSE FILL — MINOR LOOK-AHEAD ISSUE

The RSI signal is computed on today's closing prices AND the entry
fill uses today's close + slippage. In reality:
  - RSI < 30 is only knowable at the close
  - An entry at today's close is impractical (order must be placed
    BEFORE close to fill AT close)
  - Industry-realistic alternative: next-open fill

Impact: The same-day fill tends to overstate performance because
the system "enters" at the price that triggered the signal. In
trending down days (which cause RSI < 30), close is usually better
than next open. This creates a slight entry-timing advantage.

For the OOS test: this bias is UNCHANGED (same code, same window).
It will affect OOS results in the same way as in-sample — so it
does not invalidate OOS comparison, but it means absolute OOS
numbers are slightly optimistic vs real-world execution.
